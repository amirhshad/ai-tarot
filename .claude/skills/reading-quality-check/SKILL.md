# Reading Quality Check

Use this skill after generating or modifying tarot interpretation prompts or reading output. It ensures the reading meets narrative, cultural, and safety standards.

## Checklist

Run through each item before considering a reading prompt or interpretation complete:

### Narrative Cohesion
- [ ] All cards are woven into a single cohesive story (not card-by-card summaries)
- [ ] The interpretation has a clear narrative arc — opening, development, resolution
- [ ] Card positions (past/present/future or custom) shape the story structure

### Language Safety
- [ ] No deterministic or predictive language ("you will…", "this means…", "expect…")
- [ ] Uses reflective framing ("this may suggest…", "consider…", "this invites you to…")
- [ ] No alarmist tone for difficult cards (Death, Tower, Ten of Swords)
- [ ] Difficult cards are framed as transformation, release, or necessary change

### Reversal Handling
- [ ] Reversed cards are acknowledged, not ignored
- [ ] Reversals add nuance (blocked energy, internal vs external, delay) — not just "opposite"
- [ ] Reversal interpretation is integrated into the narrative, not bolted on

### Empathy & Cultural Safety
- [ ] Tone is warm, empathetic, and non-judgmental
- [ ] No assumptions about gender, relationship status, religion, or life circumstances
- [ ] Farsi readings reflect Persian cultural/literary depth (Rumi, Hafez sensibility) — not word-for-word translation
- [ ] Arabic readings respect cultural context and use appropriate literary register

### Token Length Balance
- [ ] Single card: ~150-300 words
- [ ] Three-card spread: ~400-700 words
- [ ] Custom spread: proportional to number of positions
- [ ] Follow-up responses: concise, ~100-200 words

### Multi-Language Nuance
- [ ] If non-English: concepts are culturally adapted, not literally translated
- [ ] RTL formatting is preserved in Farsi and Arabic output
- [ ] Symbolic references use culturally resonant equivalents where appropriate
